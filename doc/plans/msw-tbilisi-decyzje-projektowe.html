MŚW Tbilisi 2026 — report design decisions

What is locked about the board report, what was deliberately rejected, and what still has to be brainstormed. Written so a later session can resume without re-deriving any of it.

Status: report layout signed off. Campaign design not started.
Artifact: msw-tbilisi-raport-mockup.html (sample data, marked Makieta).
Date: 2026-07-18

Domain facts established

These came out of discussion and are not derivable from the codebase — the ranklist system does not model any of them today.

FactConsequence
Four fencers may start per country per category, plus one nominated reserve. Top 5 of each ranking is the invitation set. 5 is not arbitrary.
A fencer sits in exactly one V-category (from birth year) but may qualify in up to three weapons. One person, one message, several declarations. Individual events in different weapons do not collide, so there is no allocation problem for individual starts.
Teams combine categories: Veteran = V1+V2, Grand Veteran = V3+V4, per weapon-gender pair. 12 team events, not 24. Team readiness cannot be reported per V-category.
Team composition is not algorithmic — the fencers self-organise, and the final roster follows SPWS regulations. The report must stop at declared readiness. It must never propose a line-up.
Team start carries an additional fee. Team willingness is a separate question from individual willingness, and a fencer may legitimately say yes to one and no to the other.
The official SPWS .xlsx ranklists remain the source of truth; this system is in shadow mode. Eligibility comes from the published workbooks, not from fn_ranking_ppw. See "Naming collision" below.

Naming collision — read before writing any code

In the .xlsx workbooks, KADRA means the national-team ranking sheet. In this codebase KADRA already means something different: the combined domestic + international (+EVF) ranking mode — RankingMode = 'PPW' | 'KADRA' in frontend/src/lib/types.ts, the fn_ranking_kadra SQL function, and the i18n key kadra_title ("Ranking +EVF"). Reusing the name for this campaign will collide. Pick a distinct identifier.

Locked report design

Three sections, Polish only. Title: MŚW Tbilisi 2026 — Deklarowana chęć udziału. Header chips are limited to competition counts, deadline, and generation timestamp.

A · Proponowana reprezentacja Polski z ramienia SPWS — start indywidualny

  • Six weapon-gender groups × four categories = 24 boxes. Women's category always precedes men's.
  • Boxes top-aligned; a short list differs at the bottom, never the top.
  • One coloured row per fencer, in ranking order. No numbering, no box header, no footer line.
  • Green = confirmed, red = declined, amber = awaiting reply. Explained in the section intro, not in a swatch legend.
  • (R) marks the reserve — the fifth confirmed fencer, not rank #5. A category with only four confirmations therefore shows no reserve, which is the point.
  • Rank order alone carries the justification for the invitation; positions are never renumbered.

B · Potwierdzili chęć startu w drużynie

  • Twelve individually titled cards — <weapon> <gender> plus drużyna Veteran · V1+V2 or drużyna Grand Veteran · V3+V4.
  • Identical row styling and colour semantics to Section A.
  • No counts, no verdict lines, no proposed line-ups.
  • A team is fieldable at six or more green rows (5 + reserve). Stated in the intro so the summary KPI has a visible basis.

C · Zestawienie deklaracji zawodników

  • One row per person, deduplicated across weapons.
  • Name, birth year, age category. No club, no response metadata.
  • Per-weapon breakdown: weapon, ranking position, individual intent, team intent.

Responsive behaviour

Category boxes wrap symmetrically — four across above 1000px, 2+2 on tablet, single column below 680px. Section C collapses to per-fencer cards with each weapon as its own labelled block. Verified: no horizontal overflow at 375px.

Rejected — do not reintroduce

  • Status footers in boxes ("komplet · rezerwowy obsadzony"). The colour coding carries the message.
  • A separate Następni w rankingu footer. Superseded: a red decline row plus an extra name appended at the bottom of the list explains backfill on its own.
  • Counts and verdict lines on team cards.
  • A "Wyjazd" (travel) column. Removed from the layout entirely.
  • Colour swatch legends for status, now that the section intros explain the coding.

Decided but not yet built

Rolling waves with board-authorised backfill

Wave 1 goes to the top 5 of each ranking. Where a category falls short, lower-ranked fencers are contacted and appended to the bottom of the box. The report is a living document regenerated between waves, not a one-shot PDF — the underlying data needs a notion of invitation wave per person.

Four V4 categories in the sample have no one left to invite. The report must be able to distinguish "we have not asked" from "there is nobody left".

Open — to brainstorm next

1 · The message and its questions

Not started. What exactly is asked, in what order, how a phone reply is captured, how a fencer qualifying in three weapons answers without a wall of checkboxes, and whether travel is asked explicitly or derived from having confirmed at least one start. This is the next brainstorming topic and nothing about it has been decided.

2 · Contact data — the hard blocker

The system holds no fencer contact data, deliberately. ADR-078 states there is no email column on tbl_fencer; tbl_registration stores only txt_email_hash. No phone numbers, no Telegram handles anywhere. This campaign requires creating the first fencer-contact store, which is a GDPR-consequential decision needing its own ADR.

Possible starting point: doc/external_files/Sezon_2025-2026/XLS/czlonkowie SPWS.xlsx ("SPWS members") — not yet opened, and reading it needs explicit per-file authorisation.

3 · Outbound channel

No bulk email capability exists. The only mailer, python/pipeline/ftl_feed_seed_send.py, is Gmail SMTP built for a single organizer recipient with one attachment. ADR-079 records bulk delivery as blocked on a provider decision. Telegram is currently the only general-purpose outbound channel, and fencers are not on it.

4 · Extraction from the workbooks

Nothing in the repo fetches SPWS .xlsx files — they arrive by hand into doc/external_files/. Reusable: the filename pattern ^(SZPADA|FLORET|SZABLA)-(K?)(\d)-(\d{4}-\d{4})\.xlsx$ is already implemented in python/tools/populate_staging.py, and python/scrapers/xlsx_parser.py reads workbooks. Unknown: the exact layout of the KADRA sheet, and how many fencers qualify in more than one weapon — both answerable by parsing the 24 files, which has not been authorised yet.

Working agreements

  • Ask one plain-text question at a time, with a recommendation and its justification. Do not use the multiple-choice question UI.
  • Do not read spreadsheet contents without explicit authorisation for the named file.
  • Human-facing documents are HTML in project-local doc/plans/.
