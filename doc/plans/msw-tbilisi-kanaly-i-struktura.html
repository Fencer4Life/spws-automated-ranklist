01Messenger instead of SMS — worked through

Short answer: Messenger is the better channel and the worse mechanism. It should be in the campaign, but not in the place SMS occupies.

The hard constraint There is no legitimate way to automate outbound Messenger messages to a list of people. Meta's Messenger Platform lets a Page reply to someone who messaged it first, inside a 24-hour window — it cannot initiate. The only way to send 93 messages from your personal account is browser automation driving messenger.com, which is against Meta's terms and routinely ends in a locked account. That is your Facebook account, the same one you need for the group post that rescues the 43 unreachable. The downside isn't a failed send — it's losing the channel that the rest of the plan depends on.

And the part that has nothing to do with terms of service

A Messenger message from someone who is not your friend does not arrive in the inbox. It lands in Message Requests, a folder most people over fifty have never opened. For cold outreach the delivery rate is not 98% — it is closer to unknowable. Where you are already connected, it is excellent: read within minutes, in a place people actually look, and conversational in a way email is not.

Messenger — the human channel

add

Manual, conversational, and pointed at the two jobs SMS is bad at.

  • The 43 with no contact data — Messenger is often the only way to reach them at all, and searching 43 names by hand is a finite afternoon.
  • Asking teammates for missing contacts (your point 8) — this is a favour being asked, and a favour asked by SMS reads as spam.

Messenger as a bulk replacement for SMS

reject

Would mean automating your personal account against 93 people, most of whom would receive it in a folder they never check.

  • Worst of both: higher risk, lower delivery, no logging.
One legitimate automated Messenger path, and why I still say no You could invert the flow: the group post and the email say "message the SPWS page on Messenger and it will send you your form link". Because the fencer initiates, a Page may legitimately reply automatically inside 24 hours. It works — but it adds a step before the step, and every added step costs responses. The form link in the email is already one click. Not worth it.

03Point 8 — peer-sourcing the missing contacts

Your idea: ask the age-category teammates of an unreachable fencer to help find their phone or email. It is the right instinct — in a federation this size the social graph is far denser than any list you hold. Two things make or break it.

Batch by recipient, never by missing person The naive version loops over the 43 missing and messages everyone in their category — so an active szpada V1 fencer gets five separate messages asking about five different people, and stops reading at the second. Invert it: for each reachable person, collect every missing name in their weapon-gender-category, and send one message listing all of them. Roughly 40 messages instead of 200, and each one reads like a real request rather than a mail-merge.

What the script produces

  1. For each of the 43 unreachable, find everyone in the same weapon + gender + V-category who is reachable — these are the people who train and compete against them.
  2. Invert into one row per helper, with the list of names they are being asked about.
  3. Render a ready-to-paste Messenger message per helper, and an HTML worksheet you work down: helper, names, channel, answer.
  4. New contacts you enter go into kontakt, and the person immediately becomes eligible for the normal invitation flow — their ladder starting that day.

Two failure modes worth designing against

  • A category where nobody is reachable. If all five in a short category are missing, there is no peer to ask — the fallback is the club, or the Facebook post. The worksheet should mark those separately rather than showing an empty helper list.
  • Passing on someone's number. Normal in a small federation, but the message should say what it is for and that the data is used for this campaign only. One sentence, and it makes the request easy to say yes to.

04Point 4 — the structure your content has to fit

You write the words; this is the frame they sit in. Everything below exists so the automation can personalise, send, resend and reconcile without you touching anything twice.

Merge fields available to the email

-- identity
{imie}              Gabriela
{nazwisko}           KAMIŃSKA
{zwrot}              Pani Gabrielo / Panie Marku   -- gendered salutation, from the ranking file

-- their standing, the part that makes it not-spam
{kategoria}          V1
{lista_broni}        szpada (1. miejsce), floret (4. miejsce)
{liczba_broni}       2

-- the ask
{link}              prefilled form URL, carries {token}
{termin}             21 lipca 2026
{oplata_druzyna}     the team fee, once you provide it

-- plumbing
{token}             opaque per-person id, also the reconcile key
{wersja}             template version, recorded on every send
Why {lista_broni} matters more than it looks It is the single line that proves the message was written for that person. A veteran reading "szpada — 1. miejsce, floret — 4. miejsce" knows instantly this is real, that the federation has their results, and that the invitation is earned. It is also the cheapest spam-filter defence available: 98 genuinely different messages.

Form structure — fixed, with prefill doing the work

0. token                      prefilled, short answer
0b. imię i nazwisko           prefilled, editable — the human check

1. SZPADA   indywidualnie    tak / nie / nie dotyczy
2. SZPADA   drużyna          tak / nie / nie dotyczy
3. FLORET   indywidualnie    tak / nie / nie dotyczy
4. FLORET   drużyna          tak / nie / nie dotyczy
5. SZABLA   indywidualnie    tak / nie / nie dotyczy
6. SZABLA   drużyna          tak / nie / nie dotyczy

7. e-mail / telefon           prefilled with what we hold — free contact-list repair
8. uwagi                      free text, optional

Six fixed questions, and nie dotyczy prefilled for every weapon the person is not ranked in. A one-weapon fencer sees two live questions and four already answered; a three-weapon fencer sees six. No branching, no per-person form, one response row per person — which is what makes the reconcile trivial.

Question 7 is worth more than it costs Prefilling the contact data you hold and letting people correct it turns every response into a free repair of the contact list — and the list you are working from is visibly imperfect (an Excel-mangled phone number, an eight-digit mobile, two probable duplicates). One extra field, and by the end of the campaign you own a clean list for next time.

Templates are versioned, and every send records which one went

  • Four templates: wave1_email, wave2_resend, wave3_sms, wave4_final, plus messenger_kontakt for the peer-sourcing request.
  • Each send writes the template version into zaproszenie. When you iterate on the wording mid-campaign — and you will — you can still tell which version each person received, and whether the new one performed better.
  • The SMS template has a hard 160-character budget including the link. Worth writing to that limit deliberately rather than discovering it at send time.
