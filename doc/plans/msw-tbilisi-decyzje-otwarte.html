Blocking — needed before anything gets built

Q1blocking
Is there an SPWS Facebook group you can post in?
Asked twice, still open. 43 of 144 fencers have no email and no phone, 9 of them inside a top 5. No automation reaches them. If the group exists and you can post, that gap closes cheaply and the campaign works as designed. If not, those 26–43 names have to be chased individually through club officials, which is days of manual work and changes what "full automation" can mean.
A
Yes, I can post
Group post carries a generic form link; peers tag the missing.
B
Exists, but I can't post
Someone else posts on your behalf — needs a person and a lead time.
C
No group
Club officials become the only route. Plan for manual chasing.
NoteNo recommendation possible — this is a fact about the world, not a choice.
Q2blocking
Which address sends the mail?
Highest-leverage single choice in the campaign, and the most expensive to change after 98 people have already received the first message. Affects open rate, spam placement, and whether the request reads as official.
A recommended
SPWS-branded address
An invitation to represent Poland should not arrive from a private Gmail. Materially better open rate and far less likely to be ignored as a mistake. Worth the setup even if it takes a day.
B
Your personal Gmail
Works today, zero setup. Fine if fencers already know you as the person who runs the ranking — reads as personal rather than institutional.
Either wayReply-to should be an address you actually read — a meaningful share of answers will arrive as plain replies rather than through the form.
Q3blocking
Google Forms, or your own page served from the local stack?
The last structural choice. Determines whether the multi-weapon prefill workaround is needed at all, and where answers land.
A recommended
Google Forms → Sheet
Always up, no dependency on your machine, an interface people recognise and trust. Costs the prefill trick for multi-weapon fencers and a reconcile step into Postgres.
B
Own page via tunnel
Full control, per-person tokens, no prefill hack, answers land straight in odpowiedz. Costs a stranger-looking URL and puts campaign uptime on your machine for four weeks.
Why AThe audience skews 50+ and the difference between a recognised Google form and an unfamiliar domain is response rate, which matters more here than elegance.
Q4blocking
The message itself — never designed.
Flagged as "the next brainstorming topic" in the original design doc and still untouched. Nothing can be sent without it. What is asked, in what order, how the team question and its fee are framed, and how a three-weapon fencer answers without a wall of checkboxes.
A recommended
I draft it, you edit
I write the Polish email and the form questions as a working document; you correct tone and terminology. Fastest path to something concrete to react to.
B
Brainstorm it first
Work through the question set before any drafting. Slower, but right if you already have strong views on what must and must not be asked.
NoteThis is the largest remaining piece of work and the only one that is purely writing.

Structural — decide before the first send, not before building

Q5structural
How long is the response window?
Drives the whole escalation ladder — reminder, SMS, calls, deadline warning. I assumed 14 days when drawing the timeline; that was my assumption, not your decision.
A
10 days
Tight. Works only if the Tbilisi entry deadline forces it.
B recommended
14 days
Room for two reminders and a phone round without feeling leisurely. Holidays absorb one lost week.
C
21 days
Safer for reaching the unreachable via clubs, but long enough that early responders forget they answered.
Depends onThe actual entry deadline for Tbilisi — if you know it, that decides this rather than preference.
Q6structural
SMS — gateway, or by hand from the phone?
93 fencers have a number. SMS is the highest-open-rate second touch and the only channel for the one fencer with a phone but no email.
A recommended
Gateway (SMSAPI / SerwerSMS)
Under 15 PLN for the whole list, scriptable, logged in zaproszenie like everything else. Costs an account signup.
B
By hand from the phone
Free and immediate, but 93 messages typed by hand, unlogged, and impossible to schedule — the escalation step stops being automatic.
C
Skip SMS entirely
Email plus Facebook only. Simpler, and you lose the single most effective nudge.
Q7structural
Does the form state the team fee amount?
A team start carries an extra cost. A yes given without knowing the amount is a yes you may have to re-ask — and re-asking after someone has been counted into a team is the worst moment to discover it.
A recommended
State the amount
Even approximate, marked as such. A declaration made with the cost visible is one you can rely on.
B
Willingness in principle
Correct only if the amount genuinely is not known yet — then say exactly that in the form rather than staying silent.
