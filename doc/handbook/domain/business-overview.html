Business overview

SPWS uses one system to publish veteran fencing rankings, maintain the competition calendar and turn heterogeneous event results into trusted Polish ranking data.

Audience: all developersOwner: Product and domainVerified 12 July 2026

Value

Why the system exists

The Polish Veterans Fencing Association—SPWS—runs rankings across three weapons, two genders and five veteran age categories. Results arrive from domestic events and international providers in incompatible formats. The system gives the association one curated calendar, one fencer identity model and reproducible rankings.

The product promise: a qualifying result should reach the correct fencer, weapon, gender, age category and season ranking—and an operator must be able to understand and correct the path when source data is ambiguous.

People and systems

Actors

Fencer or visitor

Browses ranklists and calendar, inspects score composition, registers for eligible events and views entry lists.

SPWS administrator

Maintains seasons, events, tournament slots, fencer identity and scoring configuration; reviews uncertain ingestion outcomes.

Operator

Runs imports, checks reports, promotes trusted data between environments and handles recovery through workflows, scripts and Telegram.

External result provider

Publishes FencingTime, FTL, Engarde, 4Fence, Dartagnan, EVF or Ophardt data that must be normalized before it can affect rankings.

Business boundary

What the system owns

Owned

  • Season and scoring configuration
  • SPWS event calendar and tournament slots
  • Fencer master identities and known aliases
  • Registrations and consent evidence
  • Imported result provenance
  • Computed public rankings and drilldown
  • CERT-to-PROD controlled promotion

Not owned

  • Raw result-provider websites
  • Competition administration on the strip
  • Foreign federation master data
  • Email or Telegram platforms themselves
  • The host SPWS content-management system
  • Payment settlement

Domain policy

Core policy differences

Domestic intake is inclusive. SPWS owns the domestic population and aims to retain every qualifying result, including creating a provisional fencer identity when required.

International intake is selective. International ranking data is restricted to relevant Polish fencers; low-confidence identities are not blindly added to the domestic master.

Rankings remain segmented. Even when people fence in combined age or gender pools, published rankings are separated into the correct weapon, gender and veteran-category slices.

Operators curate identity and URLs. Source URLs and ambiguous identity decisions are deliberate administrative data, not disposable scraper output.

Smallest useful model

From fencing event to ranklist

  1. An event appears in the season calendar.
  2. One or more tournament slots describe the weapon, gender and age-category ranking destinations.
  3. A result source is parsed into a neutral representation.
  4. Each source name is resolved to a canonical fencer or handled according to domestic/international policy.
  5. Results are validated, split where necessary and committed transactionally.
  6. PostgreSQL ranking functions calculate points and rolling carry-over.
  7. Public views expose ranklists, calendar, score breakdowns and entry lists.
